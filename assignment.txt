# Speech AI Engineer

# Assignment

Voice Input Pipeline Design for noisy kitchen environment

# Background

You are building a voice-activated cooking assistant called Tara. The device is mounted in a home kitchen with a microphone placed on the chimney hood. Users interact with it hands-free while cooking. Your task is to build a pipeline that reliably detects and transcribes voice commands in a noisy kitchen environment.

# What you are given

- A raw audio clip recorded in a real kitchen environment with noisy background sounds (such as chimney fan, pressure cooker whistle, mixer grinder, chopping, sizzling oil) overlapping user voice.

[tara_assignment_recording_clipped.flac](attachment:40e760ba-c0ce-4dbf-97a1-2dfeaafb89f9:tara_assignment_recording_clipped.flac)

## What You Are Required To Do

**Task 1 - Build the pipeline**
Create an end-to-end pipeline that detects and processes only voice commands that start with "Hey Tara" or "Tara." The final output should be a text transcript of the recognised statements addressed to Tara, and a flow diagram of the pipeline. You can use open source models (Let us know if in case you need cloud resources) as well as closed source APIs.

**Task 2 - Document your methodology**
Create a document logging the methodology and results of every approach you tried across iterations before arriving at your final architecture.

*We want to know every technique you tried and its associated results. Your problem identification skills and the evolution of your solution carry more weightage in evaluation than the final solution itself.*

---

## What You Are Given

- One audio clip containing kitchen noise scenarios including hood fan, active frying, and multiple instances of "Hey Tara" and "Tara" commands

---

## Constraints

- End-to-end latency for the entire pipeline must be under 2 seconds, measured and documented - do not just claim it
- No single scripts or Jupyter notebooks but a well structured python package
- The STT model is allocated a maximum of 1 second of that budget. The remaining 1 second must cover all other stages combined - wake word detection, noise suppression, VAD, and any other preprocessing steps
- All pipeline stages except the STT model must be capable of running on a Raspberry Pi 5 with AI HAT+. You are not required to physically run it on the hardware, but your component choices must be justifiable for that target - document why each component fits within the compute and memory constraints of that device

---

## Submission Format

- A GitHub repository containing code files
- The flow diagram as an image file in the repository
- The methodology document as a markdown/PDF

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Listed in order of weightage:

**Methodology and problem solving** *(highest weightage)*

- Does the document show genuine iteration or a single attempt dressed up as multiple
- Does the candidate correctly identify where their pipeline will break - unprompted
- Are component choices justified with reasoning, not just named

**Wake word detection**

- Consistent detection of "Hey Tara" and "Tara" at the start of utterances
- False trigger rate on kitchen noise and words that sound similar to Tara
- False reject rate - how often valid commands are missed

**Transcript accuracy**

- Accuracy of transcribed text on the provided noisy audio clip
- Handling of utterances where "Tara" appears mid-sentence - these should not be transcribed

**Constraint adherence**

- Is measured latency documented and under 2 seconds
- Are violations of any constraint explicitly called out with justification - silent violations will be penalised more than documented ones
